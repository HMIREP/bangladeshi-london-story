Stop 7 – Truman Brewery / Save Brick Lane

How a historic brewery became ground zero for gentrification – and resistance.

What this stop covers

This stop looks at the Old Truman Brewery at the southern end of Brick Lane and the long‑running fight over plans to redevelop it into offices, retail and now a data centre with a small number of flats.[web:80][web:84][web:85]

We focus on the Save Brick Lane campaign – a coalition of local traders, residents and heritage groups – and how their organising links back to earlier struggles over housing, culture and who Brick Lane is really for.[web:82][web:89][web:91]

Key facts

  • Developers have proposed schemes including a shopping‑mall and corporate office block, and more recently a data centre plus 44 flats (only a minority for social rent).[web:80][web:85][web:84]
  • More than 7,000 objections were submitted against earlier Truman Brewery plans, alongside petitions and letters from traders and residents.[web:85][web:89]
  • In 2025 Tower Hamlets councillors voted to oppose the latest redevelopment plans; a public inquiry and legal challenges, led in part by Save Brick Lane, are still ongoing.[web:84][web:86][web:90][web:91]

Questions to bring with you

  • What does “regeneration” mean when a development risks pushing out the very communities and businesses that made Brick Lane famous?[web:88][web:92]
  • How is the Save Brick Lane campaign repeating – or updating – the tactics of BHAG and earlier anti‑gentrification struggles in the East End?[web:82][web:86][web:94]
