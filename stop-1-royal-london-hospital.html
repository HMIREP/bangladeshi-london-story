Stop 1 – Royal London Hospital / Tower Hamlets Town Hall

Beginnings: birth, migration, and how this borough becomes home.

What this stop covers

This stop connects my own birth at the Royal London Hospital, my father's journey as a merchant seaman, and the much longer history of migrant patients, workers and residents who passed through this building and this borough.

We also introduce the Quddus Ali case in the 1990s and ask how a place marked by racist violence ends up with a Bangladeshi mayor and MPs – and what that does and doesn't change about everyday life.

Key facts

  • The Royal London began life in the 18th century as one of Britain's earliest public / charitable hospitals.
  • Tower Hamlets today has one of the highest proportions of Muslim and Bangladeshi residents of any local authority in England and Wales.
  • The former hospital building now houses Tower Hamlets Town Hall, where decisions about housing, planning and services for this community are made.

Questions to bring with you

  • What happens when your own birth place is also a site of racist attacks and protests – how does that shape your sense of belonging?
  • When a hospital becomes a town hall, what really changes about who has power and who doesn't in a borough like this?
