Stop 1 – Royal London Hospital / Tower Hamlets Town Hall

Beginnings: birth, migration, and how this borough becomes home.

What this stop covers

This stop connects my own birth at the Royal London Hospital, my father’s journey as a merchant seaman, and the much longer history of migrant patients, workers and residents who passed through this building and this borough.[file:43]

We also introduce the Quddus Ali case in the 1990s and ask how a place marked by racist violence ends up with a Bangladeshi mayor and MPs – and what that does and doesn’t change about everyday life.[file:43]

Key facts

  • The Royal London began life in the 18th century as one of Britain’s earliest public / charitable hospitals.[file:43]
  • Tower Hamlets today has one of the highest proportions of Muslim and Bangladeshi residents of any local authority in England and Wales.[file:43]
  • The former hospital building now houses Tower Hamlets Town Hall, where decisions about housing, planning and services for this community are made.[file:43]

Questions to bring with you

  • What happens when your own birth place is also a site of racist attacks and protests – how does that shape your sense of belonging?[file:43]
  • When a hospital becomes a town hall, what really changes about who has power and who doesn’t in a borough like this?[file:43]
