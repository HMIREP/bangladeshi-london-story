About the Historian

I was born in Whitechapel in 1967, at the very hospital where our journey begins, Royal London Hospital (now the Town Hall). My story is tied to the streets of the East End, following in the footsteps of my father who arrived as a merchant seaman in the 1940s.

As an activist during the pivotal 1990s, I helped organize rallies—including the Quddus Ali rally in 1993—that fought for our community's safety and presence. With a background in language teaching, theatre and history (Cambridge University) I lead this seminar to ensure the "lived memory" of our resistance isn't lost to gentrification.
