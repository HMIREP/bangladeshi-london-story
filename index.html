8 Iconic Bangladeshi London Stops

1. Brick Lane

Curry capital of UK, Banglatown heart. Street art, vintage shops, 100+ restaurants. Green/red lampposts = Bangladesh flag colors. [web:242]

2. Whitechapel Market

Daily Bangladeshi life - fresh fish, sari shops, halal meat. Bengali signs everywhere. Vibrant street market since 1970s. [web:243]

3. Altab Ali Park

Memorial to murdered Bangladeshi worker (1978). Shaheed Minar replica honors martyrs. Anti-racism landmark. [web:242][web:246]

4. Brick Lane Mosque

Huguenot chapel → Jewish synagogue → now mosque. Color-changing LED minaret. Symbol of East End immigration waves. [web:242][web:254]

5. East London Mosque

1941 community hub. Largest in UK. Architectural landmark in Whitechapel. [web:245]

6. Kobi Nazrul Centre

Bengali arts hub. Exhibitions, plays, rebel poet namesake. Cultural heartbeat. [web:242]

7. Sunday Upmarket

Brick Lane food hall. Largest halal street food selection. Bangladeshi flavors + global fusion. [web:242]

8. Shadwell & Toynbee Hall

Early Bengali settlement (1880s lascars). Social reform history. Modern regeneration. [web:246][web:248]

About Banglatown

Brick Lane = Bangladeshi heart of London since 1970s. From Sylheti migrants fleeing Bangladesh war to today's thriving community of 80,000+. Tower Hamlets home to UK's largest Bangladeshi population. Curry houses generated £90M economy. Cultural fusion with street art, markets, mosques. [web:245][web:251]
