Here to Stay

A British-Bangladeshi London Story

A Guided Walking Field Seminar

This digital narrative serves as the archival companion to the physical walking seminar, providing primary-source context for each stage of our field investigation. The seminar develops historiographical thinking—examining not just what happened, but how communities interpret, contest, and author their own histories.

About the Historian

Hussain Mohamed Ismail

I was born in Whitechapel in 1967, at the Royal London Hospital—the very site where our seminar begins. My narrative is deeply rooted in the East End, following the footsteps of my father who arrived as a merchant seaman in the 1940s.

As an activist during the transformative 1990s, I helped organise pivotal community defences, including the 1993 Quddus Ali rally. With a background in language teaching, theatre, and history from Cambridge University, I lead this seminar to ensure the lived memory of our resistance is preserved within the changing urban landscape.

I provide these sessions as a platform for critical engagement with London's social history, blending academic rigour with first-hand community insights.

Inquire

I offer this specialist field seminar only on request for university departments, school groups, and professional heritage researchers. I can also conduct this seminar in Spanish, Italian or Portuguese.
