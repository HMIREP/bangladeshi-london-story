About the Historian

I was born in Whitechapel in 1967, at the Royal London Hospital—the very site where our seminar begins. My narrative is deeply rooted in the East End, following the footsteps of my father who arrived as a merchant seaman in the 1940s.

As an activist during the transformative 1990s, I helped organize pivotal community defenses, including the 1993 Quddus Ali rally. With a background in language teaching, theatre, and history from Cambridge University, I lead this seminar to ensure the lived memory of our resistance is preserved within the changing urban landscape.
